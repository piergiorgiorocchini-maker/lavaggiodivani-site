Quick checklist

What to check before the yacht is guest-ready

Pre-charter cleaning is not just about making the interior look presentable in photos. Textile surfaces hold dust, odours, humidity, residues and stains in places that guests notice immediately, especially during a final turnaround before charter or owner arrival.

Carpets and fitted moquette

Check saloon carpets, stairs, corridors, cabin floors and high-traffic areas for dark lanes, salt residue, drink spills and old spot treatments.

Upholstery and seating

Inspect sofas, cushions, dining seating and guest-facing upholstery for body oils, sun cream marks, wine stains, odours and uneven fabric colour.

Mattresses and cabins

Check mattress freshness, cabin ventilation, humidity signs, odours and textiles that have stayed closed for long periods between trips.

Drying and access

Confirm realistic drying time, ventilation, marina access, parking, onboard working space and the order of work around crew activity.

Before booking

Start with the areas guests actually notice

On a yacht, not every textile has the same priority before charter. The most visible and most used zones should come first: saloon seating, dining areas, cabin mattresses, fitted carpets, stairs and any textile surface close to humidity or odour sources.

1

Walk the guest route

Start where guests board, then move through the saloon, cabins, bathrooms, stairs and guest seating. Look for marks that are visible at normal standing height, not only close-up.

2

Separate cosmetic issues from hygiene issues

A small visible mark and a persistent odour are different problems. Odour, humidity and mattress freshness often matter more than a tiny spot hidden under a cushion.

3

Check timing before promising results

Cleaning, drying and ventilation need practical time. A rushed intervention can be useful, but it should be planned honestly around charter schedule, access and cabin airflow.

Common pre-charter problems

Textile issues that should be assessed before guests arrive

Wine and drink stains

Red wine, coffee and drink spills should be assessed before aggressive products are used. Rubbing or bleaching can set the stain, spread it or damage the fibres.

Closed-cabin odours

Cabins closed for weeks can develop stale textile odours, especially where humidity, mattresses, upholstery and poor airflow meet in a small space.

High-traffic carpet lanes

Stairs, corridors and saloon walkways often show dark traffic lanes. These areas usually need more attention than low-use corners that still look presentable.

Mattress freshness

Mattresses deserve a specific check before charter, especially after previous use, storage periods, humidity or any cabin that does not smell fully fresh.

Mistakes to avoid

Do not make the textile problem worse before help arrives

Some textile damage happens before the cleaning appointment, not during it. The safest approach is to avoid aggressive treatment, document the issue and request a realistic assessment before using products onboard.

1

Do not scrub aggressively

Scrubbing can push stains deeper, distort fibres or create a wider halo around the original mark.

2

Do not mix random products

Bleach, household detergents, stain removers and disinfectants can react badly with marine textiles or previous treatments. For fabrics such as Sunbrella, the manufacturer’s marine upholstery guidance recommends blotting liquid spills, using mild soap and water when appropriate, rinsing thoroughly and allowing fabric to air dry. Always check the actual textile type before applying any product. Sunbrella marine upholstery cleaning guidance.

3

Do not over-wet cushions or mattresses

Too much moisture in closed yacht interiors can create drying problems, odours and humidity issues, especially under time pressure.

4

Do not wait until the last hour

Emergency work is sometimes possible, but pre-charter textile care is better when there is time for assessment, cleaning, ventilation and drying.

FAQ

Questions before booking pre-charter yacht interior cleaning

How far before charter should yacht interior textiles be cleaned?

Timing depends on the textile, humidity, ventilation and the level of cleaning required. When possible, plan the assessment before the final turnaround day so carpets, upholstery and mattresses have enough time to dry and settle before guests arrive.

Can I send photos before booking yacht carpet or upholstery cleaning?

Yes. Photos of the saloon, cabins, carpets, upholstery, stains and textile labels help with a first practical assessment before confirming availability and the type of intervention.

Do you cover Porto Cervo, Olbia, Portisco and Porto Rotondo?

The yacht textile cleaning service is organised for Sardinia and key yachting areas including Costa Smeralda, Porto Cervo, Olbia, Portisco and Porto Rotondo, subject to availability and job details.

Can all stains be removed before charter?

Not every stain can be guaranteed, especially if it is old, chemically treated or on delicate fabric. A photo assessment helps set realistic expectations before anyone makes the stain worse with unsuitable products.

Is mattress sanitisation useful before guest arrival?

Mattress sanitisation can be useful before charter, seasonal reopening or owner arrival, especially after humidity, closed cabins, odours or heavy previous use.

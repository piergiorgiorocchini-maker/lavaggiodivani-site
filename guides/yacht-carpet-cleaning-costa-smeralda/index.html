Quick checklist

Carpet details to inspect before booking

Yacht carpets are not just decorative surfaces. They collect salt residue, dust, drink spills, shoe marks, humidity and traffic lanes in the exact areas guests notice first.

Saloon traffic lanes

Check walkways, dining areas and seating zones where shoes, drinks and repeated crew movement create darker lines.

Cabin carpets

Look for stale odours, humidity marks and carpet edges close to wardrobes, bathrooms or low-ventilation corners.

Stairs and corridors

These areas often carry the most visible dirt because they connect guest and crew movement.

Old spot treatments

Identify areas where products have already been used. Prior treatment can change the result and should be mentioned before booking.

Before booking

Photograph the carpet as a working surface, not as decoration

A useful assessment needs context. A close-up of the stain is helpful, but it is not enough. The technician also needs to understand layout, access, surface size, ventilation and timing before charter.

1

Start with wide photos

Take one wide photo of the saloon or cabin so the carpet size, access and surrounding furniture are clear.

2

Add problem close-ups

Send close photos of stains, dark lanes, edges, stairs, corridors or odour-prone areas.

3

Mention timing and marina access

Before charter, access and drying time matter as much as the carpet condition. Include location, schedule and practical access notes.

Common problems

Yacht carpet issues that deserve early assessment

Wine and drink spills

Red wine, coffee and cocktails should be assessed before rubbing, bleaching or aggressive treatment makes the stain harder to improve.

Salt and shoe residue

Salt air, wet soles and marina traffic can leave dull areas that look worse under interior lighting.

Closed-cabin odour

Carpet, mattresses and upholstery can trap stale cabin odours after periods of humidity or low ventilation.

Delicate fitted moquette

Fitted moquette needs realistic expectations because access, edges, adhesives and surrounding finishes can limit aggressive cleaning.

What to photograph

Useful photos before a carpet cleaning quote

Good photos reduce uncertainty before scheduling. Send wide images, close-ups and location context so the assessment does not rely on guesswork, humanity’s favourite project management method.

Field notes

Practical notes before yacht carpet cleaning

Before booking yacht carpet cleaning in Costa Smeralda, it is useful to separate visible appearance, odour, drying conditions and charter schedule.

Drying is part of the job

Ventilation, humidity and time before guest arrival affect the result and the booking window.

Not every mark is the same

Traffic lanes, drink stains, salt residue and old product marks require different expectations.

Do not over-wet the area

Trying to solve the problem with excessive water onboard can create drying and odour problems.

Use fabric care guidance

For marine upholstery and fabrics, manufacturer care guidance can be useful before using products. Sunbrella publishes marine fabric cleaning guidance for reference: Sunbrella marine upholstery cleaning guide.

FAQ

Questions before booking yacht carpet cleaning in Costa Smeralda

When should yacht carpets be cleaned before charter?

When possible, assess them before the final turnaround day so cleaning, drying and ventilation can be organised without pressure.

Can I send photos of yacht carpets before booking?

Yes. Wide photos, close-ups of stains and notes about marina access help with the first practical assessment.

Do you cover Porto Cervo, Portisco and Porto Rotondo?

The yacht textile cleaning service is organised for Costa Smeralda and key yachting areas including Porto Cervo, Portisco and Porto Rotondo, subject to availability.

Can all carpet stains be removed?

No guarantee is possible without seeing the textile and stain history. Old stains, aggressive previous treatments and delicate fibres can limit the result.

Is fitted moquette different from loose carpet?

Yes. Fitted moquette can be limited by edges, adhesives, surrounding finishes and onboard access, so expectations should be realistic.
